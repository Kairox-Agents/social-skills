# Ideation Frameworks

> 15 repeatable frameworks for generating content ideas. Each includes when to use it, step-by-step instructions, and an example output.

---

## 1. Pillar Rotation

**When:** Weekly planning. Ensures topic diversity across posts.

**Steps:**
1. Define 3–5 content pillars (e.g., educational, personal, opinion, behind-the-scenes, promotional)
2. Assign a ratio (40/25/20/10/5 is a solid default)
3. For each slot in your calendar, rotate through pillars in ratio order
4. Within each pillar, brainstorm 3 angles before picking one

**Example:** 5 posts/week on X → 2 educational, 1 personal, 1 opinion, 1 behind-the-scenes.

---

## 2. Pain Point Mining

**When:** You need content that resonates emotionally and drives saves/shares.

**Steps:**
1. List your audience's top 10 frustrations (from DMs, comments, Reddit, reviews)
2. For each pain point, write the sentence they'd say to a friend at a bar
3. That sentence = your hook. The solution = your content body.
4. Prioritize pain points by frequency (how often you see it mentioned)

**Example:** Pain: "I post every day but nothing gets engagement." → Post: "The myth of 'just be consistent' (and what actually works instead)"

---

## 3. Format-First Ideation

**When:** You're stuck on topics but know what format performs well.

**Steps:**
1. Pick a high-performing format (thread, carousel, listicle, story post)
2. List 10 topics that fit that format's structure
3. Filter by: "Would I save this if someone else posted it?"
4. Pick the top 3

**Example:** Format = numbered list thread → "7 tools I use every day", "5 mistakes I made in year one", "9 hooks that always work"

---

## 4. Reverse Engineering

**When:** You want to replicate what's already working in your niche.

**Steps:**
1. Find 5 accounts in your niche with high engagement
2. Sort their posts by engagement (likes + comments + shares)
3. For each top post, identify: hook type, format, topic angle, CTA
4. Write your version with YOUR perspective and voice
5. Never copy — extract the pattern, add your experience

**Example:** Competitor's viral post: "10 things I learned raising $5M" → Your version: "10 things I learned bootstrapping to $500K (no VC edition)"

---

## 5. 10x Headlines

**When:** You have a topic but need the right angle to make it compelling.

**Steps:**
1. Write your topic as a boring headline ("How to write better hooks")
2. Write 10 more versions, each more specific or provocative than the last
3. Apply constraints: add a number, add a timeframe, add a contrarian twist, add a personal story, add specificity
4. Pick the one that makes YOU want to click

**Example progression:**
1. "How to write better hooks"
2. "7 hook formulas that stop the scroll"
3. "I tested 7 hook formulas for 30 days — here's what actually worked"
4. "The hook formula that got me 10x my normal reach (and it's embarrassingly simple)"

---

## 6. Audience Questions

**When:** You want content that directly answers what your audience is asking.

**Steps:**
1. Collect questions from: DMs, comments, reply threads, Reddit, Quora, "People Also Ask" on Google
2. Group similar questions into themes
3. For each theme, create one definitive post that answers the core question
4. Use the exact language your audience uses (not your jargon)

**Example:** Question from DMs: "How often should I post on LinkedIn?" → Post: "The real answer to 'how often should I post' (it depends on exactly 3 things)"

---

## 7. Trend Jacking

**When:** Something is trending in your industry or in culture broadly, and you have a genuine take.

**Steps:**
1. Monitor trends: X trending, Google Trends, Reddit front page, industry newsletters
2. Ask: "Does this trend connect to my niche in a non-obvious way?"
3. If yes: write the take within 4 hours (speed matters for trends)
4. If the connection is forced, skip it — forced trend jacking reads as desperate

**Example:** Trending: "AI is replacing copywriters" → Your take: "I used AI to write 50 LinkedIn posts. Here's why I went back to writing them myself."

---

## 8. Contrarian Angles

**When:** You see consensus in your niche and genuinely disagree (or can play devil's advocate).

**Steps:**
1. List 5 things "everyone agrees on" in your niche
2. For each, ask: "Is this actually true? What's the counterargument?"
3. Only write the contrarian take if you can back it up with experience or data
4. Frame as "unpopular opinion" or "hot take" to signal you know it's against consensus

**Example:** Consensus: "You should niche down." → Contrarian: "Niching down killed my growth. Here's what I did instead."

---

## 9. Case Study Mining

**When:** You have results (yours or someone else's) worth analyzing publicly.

**Steps:**
1. Pick a specific outcome (revenue milestone, growth spike, failed launch)
2. Identify the 3 key decisions that led to that outcome
3. Structure as: situation → decision → result → lesson
4. Include real numbers when possible (specificity = credibility)

**Example:** "How we went from 0 to 10K followers in 90 days (with a $0 budget): the 3 things that actually moved the needle"

---

## 10. Number Posts

**When:** You want high save/bookmark rates. Lists perform consistently across all platforms.

**Steps:**
1. Pick a topic your audience cares about
2. Choose a number: odd numbers (7, 9, 11) outperform even ones; specific numbers (37, 143) create curiosity
3. Each item must be independently valuable (someone could screenshot just that one)
4. Order: start strong, put the weakest in the middle, end with the best

**Example:** "11 Chrome extensions that save me 5+ hours per week"

---

## 11. Before/After

**When:** You want to show transformation — yours or your audience's.

**Steps:**
1. Define the "before" state in vivid, specific terms
2. Define the "after" state with equal specificity
3. Identify the bridge (what changed? what action/insight?)
4. Format as: before → bridge → after
5. Works as text, carousel, or video

**Example:** "Before: spending 4 hours writing one LinkedIn post, second-guessing every word. After: writing 5 posts in 1 hour using one simple framework. The bridge: I stopped trying to be original and started using proven formulas."

---

## 12. Behind-the-Scenes

**When:** You want to build trust and show the "real" version of your work.

**Steps:**
1. Pick a process your audience is curious about (your workflow, tools, decision-making)
2. Show the messy version — not the polished outcome
3. Be specific: "I use [tool] for [reason]" > "I have a great workflow"
4. Include one thing that went wrong or that you'd do differently

**Example:** "My actual content creation workflow (including the part where I stare at a blank doc for 20 minutes)"

---

## 13. Industry Commentary

**When:** Something happened in your industry (acquisition, product launch, controversy, data release).

**Steps:**
1. Summarize the event in 1–2 sentences
2. Give YOUR analysis: what does this mean? who benefits? who loses?
3. Predict what happens next
4. Ask your audience for their take

**Example:** "[Company] just launched [product]. Here's why I think this changes [specific thing] for [audience]. But I might be wrong — what's your read?"

---

## 14. Prediction Posts

**When:** You have a strong thesis about where your industry is heading.

**Steps:**
1. Make a specific prediction with a timeframe
2. Support it with 2–3 observable trends
3. Acknowledge what could make you wrong
4. Invite others to make their predictions

**Example:** "By end of 2027, 80% of SaaS content marketing will be AI-generated. The 20% that's human-written will outperform by 10x. Here's why..."

---

## 15. Reaction Posts

**When:** Someone influential posted something you agree with, disagree with, or can expand on.

**Steps:**
1. Quote or reference the original post (with attribution)
2. State your reaction clearly: agree, disagree, "yes AND..."
3. Add your unique perspective or data
4. Works especially well as Quote Tweets, LinkedIn reposts with commentary, or Reddit replies

**Example:** Reacting to a viral tweet about hustle culture: "I saw [person]'s post about working 80-hour weeks. Here's what they're not telling you about the tradeoffs..."

---

## Meta-Framework: The 3-3-3 Sprint

When you need a week's worth of content in one sitting:

1. **3 minutes** — brain dump every topic you could write about (no filtering)
2. **3 topics** — pick the 3 best from your dump
3. **3 angles** — for each topic, write 3 different hooks/angles
4. **Pick the best angle for each** and write it

Total time: 30–60 minutes for a full week of content seeds.

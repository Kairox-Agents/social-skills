# Anti-Patterns — The Cringe Detector

Load this file before generating any social content. If the output matches ANY of these patterns, rewrite.

## Universal AI-Tell Patterns (All Platforms)

### Opening Lines That Scream "AI Wrote This"
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "Here's the thing..."
- "Let me be honest..."
- "I just wanted to share..."
- "It's no secret that..."
- "As a [title], I can tell you..."
- "This might be controversial, but..."
- "Unpopular opinion:" followed by a popular opinion
- "I've been thinking a lot about..."
- "The truth is..."

### Structural Tells
- Perfect parallel structure in every bullet point
- Exactly 5 items in every list (AI default)
- Em-dash overuse (—) where a comma or period works
- Synonym cycling ("important, significant, crucial, vital")
- Hedging stack ("might potentially possibly help")
- "Firstly... Secondly... Thirdly..." mechanical progression
- Every paragraph is exactly 2-3 sentences (too uniform)
- Closing with "In conclusion" or "To summarize"

### Vocabulary Tells
- "Leverage" (say "use")
- "Game-changer" (say what actually changed)
- "Disruptive" / "Innovative" (show, don't label)
- "Synergy" (just don't)
- "Cutting-edge" (describe the edge)
- "Passionate about" (show the passion)
- "Deep dive" (when it's actually surface-level)
- "Unlock" as a verb for abstract concepts
- "Navigate" when you mean "deal with"
- "Landscape" when you mean "market" or "situation"
- "Ecosystem" for anything that's not biology
- "Holistic" (usually meaningless)
- "Actionable insights" (be the insight instead)

### Tone Tells
- Corporate formality when casual is appropriate
- Balanced-to-a-fault "on one hand... on the other hand" without taking a stance
- Excessive positivity without substance
- Motivational quotes without personal context
- Perfect grammar when the platform rewards imperfection

## Platform-Specific Cringe

### X/Twitter Cringe
- 🚀💰🔥 emoji triplet (crypto bro / engagement farmer)
- "Agree?" as the entire CTA
- "RT if you agree, Like if you disagree" (engagement farming, algorithm detects this)
- Threads that start with "I spent 100 hours researching [topic]. Here's what I found:" (overused format)
- "Thread 🧵" in first tweet (the 🧵 alone is fine; "Thread 🧵" is filler)
- Starting every tweet in a thread with a number AND emoji: "1/ 🎯" "2/ 💡" "3/ 🔥"
- Scheduling 10 tweets at perfectly even 2-hour intervals (looks automated)

### LinkedIn Cringe
- "I'm humbled and honored to announce..."
- "Agree? 🙏" (engagement bait, actively suppressed)
- "Day 1 at [company]!" without any actual insight
- Humble-brags disguised as gratitude
- "I was sitting at my desk when..." dramatic storytelling that goes nowhere
- "3 lessons I learned from [famous person]" without having met them
- Buzzword bingo: "synergy," "paradigm shift," "disrupting"
- The broem format (every sentence is a one-word paragraph): "Stop. \n Think. \n Act." (overplayed)
- "#Leadership #Innovation #Growth #Mindset #Entrepreneurship" (hashtag spam)

### Reddit Cringe
- Any hashtag (Reddit doesn't have hashtags)
- Any emoji in the title
- Marketing language ("We're excited to share...")
- "Check out my [product]!" (promotional death sentence)
- "As a [credentials]..." in place of actual content
- Professional bio-style usernames
- Cross-posting the same content to 5 subreddits simultaneously
- "Edit: Thanks for the gold, kind stranger!" (dated)
- "EDIT: Wow, this blew up!" (nobody cares about your surprise)

### Instagram Cringe
- 30 hashtags (limit is 5 now, and stuffing was always ugly)
- "Link in bio! 👆" when there's no link
- Motivational quotes on stock photo backgrounds
- "Double tap if you agree!" (engagement bait)
- Overly filtered, AI-generated "perfect" imagery (Mosseri says raw > polished)
- Captions that are just emoji strings
- #follow4follow #like4like

### TikTok Cringe
- "Hey guys! Welcome back to my channel!" (immediate swipe)
- Corporate production quality (looks like an ad, not a person)
- 🚀💰💪 in caption
- "Watch till the end for a surprise!" (nobody trusts this)
- Watermarks from other platforms
- Copying a trend that peaked 2 weeks ago
- Static text-on-screen with no audio strategy

### YouTube Cringe
- "SMASH that like button!" (dated, viewers tune this out)
- 5-minute intro before any value
- Clickbait thumbnail that doesn't match content
- "What's up guys it's ya boy [name] back at it again" (2018 energy)
- Asking for subscribers in the first 10 seconds (earn it first)

## The "Human Test"

Before finalizing any piece of content, ask:

1. Would a real person in this niche actually say this?
2. Would you cringe if you saw this on your own feed?
3. Could you attribute this to 10 different people? (If yes, it's too generic.)
4. Does it have a genuine opinion, or is it "both-sides" fluff?
5. Does it use specific examples, or is it all abstract advice?

If the answer to any of 1-5 is wrong, rewrite.

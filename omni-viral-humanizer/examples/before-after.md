# Before / After Example

## Input

"Most founders post content but get no results. They copy trends and sound like everyone else."

## Humanized base

"Most founders aren't losing on content because they post too little. They're losing because every post sounds interchangeable."

## X (single)

Most founders aren't invisible.

They're interchangeable.

If your post could've been written by 10 other people in your niche, nobody remembers it.

What's one opinion you believe that most founders in your space avoid saying out loud?

## LinkedIn (text)

Most founders don’t fail at content because of consistency.

They fail because their voice is generic.

If your post could be copied, pasted, and attributed to 20 other people in your category, it won’t build trust.

The fix is not “post more.”
The fix is point-of-view density:
- stronger first 3 lines
- one concrete lesson
- one specific example
- one clear opinion

What’s one unpopular truth you’ve learned while building your company?

## TikTok (script)

Hook (0-3s): "Your content isn't failing because you're inconsistent."
Beat 1: "It's failing because you sound like everyone else in your niche."
Beat 2: "If people can swap your name with another founder's, you've got no edge."
Beat 3: "One post = one strong opinion + one proof point + one clear takeaway."
CTA: "Comment the unpopular truth you believe about your industry."
Caption: "Why founder content flops (and how to fix it) #founder #contentstrategy #startuptips"

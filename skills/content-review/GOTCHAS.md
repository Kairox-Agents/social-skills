# Gotchas — Content Review

## 1. Passing Platform Compliance ≠ Good Content

**What happens:** A post passes all Stage 1 checks (character count, hashtag limit, format compliance) but is still generic, voiceless, or boring. The reviewer marks it as "PASS" because it's technically correct.

**Why it matters:** Platform compliance is table stakes. A 275-character tweet with 0 hashtags and no links can still be terrible content. Stage 2 (taste and quality) must NEVER be skipped just because Stage 1 passes.

**How to avoid:** Always run both stages. If time-pressured, Stage 2 matters more than Stage 1 — it's better to post great content with a minor spec violation than spec-perfect content with no soul.

## 2. Over-Editing Kills Voice

**What happens:** Reviewer iterates 4-5 times, polishing grammar, tightening sentences, and removing "imperfections." Each round makes the content more technically perfect and less human.

**Why it matters:** Social media voice REQUIRES imperfections. Fragments, casual punctuation, starting sentences with "And" or "But" — these are features, not bugs. Over-polished content triggers AI-tell detection on LinkedIn (30% less reach for AI-sounding content per LinkedIn AI Study 2026).

**How to avoid:** Limit review to 2 rounds maximum. On the third round, stop. If it's still not right, it's a rewrite problem, not a review problem. Preserve deliberate imperfections.

## 3. The "Cringe Blind Spot"

**What happens:** Reviewer misses cringe because the content sounds professional and well-structured. But "professional and well-structured" IS the cringe on social platforms — it reads as corporate or AI-generated.

**Examples that pass technical review but should fail cringe:**
- "I'm thrilled to announce our latest milestone..." (LinkedIn AI-tell)
- "5 Key Takeaways From My Experience..." (generic template)
- "In today's fast-paced digital landscape..." (AI opening)

**How to avoid:** Run the anti-patterns.md checklist on EVERY piece. The cringe score (Stage 2) should be the hardest score to earn a 5/5.

## 4. Batch Review Fatigue

**What happens:** Reviewing 15+ posts for a weekly content calendar. By post 8, the reviewer is rubber-stamping. Quality drops on later posts because attention drops.

**Why it matters:** The posts you schedule for later in the week are often the ones that need the most attention (they're the ones you wrote when YOU were fatigued too).

**How to avoid:** Review in batches of 5 max. Take a break between batches. Review the last post in the batch as carefully as the first.

## 5. Platform-Specific Rules Change Faster Than Review Checklists

**What happens:** Reviewer checks against last year's Instagram hashtag limit (30) instead of the 2026 limit (5). Or doesn't know that LinkedIn's 360Brew now penalizes engagement pod patterns, or that X's Grok-powered algorithm does sentiment analysis.

**Why it matters:** Platform rules change quarterly. A checklist from 6 months ago may have stale rules. Instagram's hashtag limit dropped from 30 to 5. LinkedIn's entire algorithm was replaced with 360Brew. X added Grok sentiment analysis.

**How to avoid:** Always reload the platform reference file from `social-context/references/platforms/<platform>.md` before reviewing. These files are the source of truth and should be updated quarterly.

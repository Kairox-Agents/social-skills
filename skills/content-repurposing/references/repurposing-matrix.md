# Repurposing Matrix

> For every source format × target format pair: what to extract, how to adapt, expected output.

## Source Formats
- Blog post (500–3000 words)
- Podcast transcript (20–60 min)
- Video transcript (5–60 min)
- Newsletter issue
- Presentation / slide deck

## Target Formats
X single tweet · X thread · LinkedIn post · LinkedIn carousel · Reddit post · Instagram caption · Instagram carousel · TikTok script · YouTube Short script · YouTube community post

---

## Blog Post → Targets

### Blog → X Single Tweet
**Extract:** The single most surprising or counterintuitive sentence in the post.
**Adapt:** Strip context. Lead with the claim. 1-2 sentences max. No link in the post body (add as reply).
**Expected output:** 120–240 chars, one strong statement or question.
**Watch out for:** Pulling a "setup" line that needs context to land.

### Blog → X Thread
**Extract:** Each H2 or major argument becomes one tweet. Intro tweet = the top-line insight.
**Adapt:** Number the tweets. Each tweet must stand alone. Trim examples ruthlessly. Final tweet = CTA or summary.
**Expected output:** 5–12 tweets, each 180–260 chars.
**Watch out for:** Over-explaining. If a tweet needs more than 2 sentences, split it.

### Blog → LinkedIn Post
**Extract:** The post's central tension or story (why did you write this? what changed?).
**Adapt:** Open with a hook that creates a "see more" click. Add a personal lens. Format with line breaks every 1–2 sentences. End with a genuine question.
**Expected output:** 800–2,200 chars, personal voice, no bullet lists in the first 210 chars.
**Watch out for:** Starting with "I wrote a blog post about..." — that's a summary, not content.

### Blog → LinkedIn Carousel
**Extract:** The numbered list, framework, or step-by-step from the post.
**Adapt:** Slide 1 = hook (same as a LinkedIn post hook). Slides 2–N = one point per slide. Final slide = CTA + author name.
**Expected output:** 5–12 slides. Each slide: 1 headline + 1–3 supporting lines.
**Watch out for:** Slides that require reading the previous slide to make sense.

### Blog → Reddit Post
**Extract:** The most useful, specific, practical insight. Anything with data or original research.
**Adapt:** Lead with value, not yourself. Rewrite in second person ("here's how" not "I found"). Strip brand mentions. Invite discussion.
**Expected output:** 300–800 words, no headers, conversational tone.
**Watch out for:** ANY self-promotional language — instant downvotes.

### Blog → Instagram Caption
**Extract:** The emotional core — what does this content make someone feel or realize?
**Adapt:** Open with a 1-sentence hook. 3–5 line breaks. Short sentences. Hashtags at the end (5–10 relevant ones). CTA = save or share.
**Expected output:** 150–400 chars visible before "more", 500–1500 chars total.
**Watch out for:** Copying the blog intro — too formal, too long.

### Blog → Instagram Carousel
**Extract:** Same as LinkedIn carousel but optimize for visual impact over information density.
**Adapt:** Slide 1 = bold visual hook (fewer words, bigger idea). Use bold claims over detailed explanations. Visual consistency across slides.
**Expected output:** 5–10 slides, 10–15 words per slide max.
**Watch out for:** Too much text per slide — Instagram carousels are skimmed, not read.

### Blog → TikTok Script
**Extract:** The one "aha moment" from the post — the thing that makes people think "I never thought of it that way."
**Adapt:** Hook in first 3 seconds (spoken). Use "you" language. Tell it as a story or demonstration. No lists — narrative flow only.
**Expected output:** 150–300 words, 60–90 seconds when spoken, conversational.
**Watch out for:** Bullet points and headers — rewrite as spoken sentences.

### Blog → YouTube Short Script
**Extract:** One specific tip, stat, or claim that can be shown or demonstrated.
**Adapt:** Hook (3s) → problem (5s) → insight (30s) → payoff (10s). Visual-first — script the action, not just the words.
**Expected output:** 100–200 words, 45–60 seconds, show-don't-tell.
**Watch out for:** Scripts that work as text but need visuals to land on video.

### Blog → YouTube Community Post
**Extract:** A teaser, poll question, or preview that drives engagement without giving everything away.
**Adapt:** Short (100–300 chars). Ask a question or share a stat. Link to the full blog/video.
**Expected output:** 1–3 sentences + optional poll.
**Watch out for:** Just posting the blog title — no hook, no reason to click.

## Podcast Transcript → Targets

### Podcast → X Thread
**Extract:** The guest's most quotable moment + the key insight per segment.
**Adapt:** Tweet 1 = best quote (attributed). Tweets 2–N = one insight per major topic. Final tweet = episode link (as reply or final thread tweet to avoid suppression).
**Expected output:** 6–10 tweets.

### Podcast → LinkedIn Post
**Extract:** The story arc — the problem the guest faced, what changed, what they learned.
**Adapt:** Retell as a narrative ("I was talking to [name] and they said something that stopped me cold..."). First person.
**Expected output:** 800–2,000 chars.

### Podcast → Blog Post
**Extract:** Full transcript → structure into H2s → flesh out each section.
**Adapt:** Add context, links, examples. Rewrite from transcript voice to editorial voice.
**Expected output:** 1,200–3,000 words.

### Podcast → Instagram Carousel
**Extract:** 5–8 most quotable/actionable lines.
**Adapt:** One quote per slide. Consistent visual template. Speaker attribution on each slide.
**Expected output:** 6–10 slides.

### Podcast → TikTok Script
**Extract:** One clip-worthy exchange — a moment of revelation, disagreement, or strong opinion.
**Adapt:** Start in the middle of the action ("So I asked them why they failed..."). Cut setup. End on the insight.
**Expected output:** 60–90 seconds of spoken content.

## Video Transcript → Targets

### Video → X Thread
**Extract:** Each chapter/section title + the core claim.
**Adapt:** Same as podcast → X thread. Each tweet = one chapter's insight.
**Expected output:** 5–8 tweets.

### Video → LinkedIn Post
**Extract:** The "why I made this video" story — the original problem, the discovery.
**Adapt:** Tell the behind-the-scenes story. "I made a video about [topic] but the real insight was..."
**Expected output:** 600–1,500 chars.

### Video → Blog Post
**Extract:** Full transcript → clean up filler words → add headers → expand thin sections.
**Adapt:** Add links, screenshots, timestamps. Rewrite spoken sentences into written ones.
**Expected output:** 1,000–2,500 words.

### Video → TikTok/Short Script
**Extract:** The single most "wait, what?" moment from the video.
**Adapt:** Clip it (if possible) or re-record as a standalone 60-second version.
**Expected output:** 60–90 second script.

## Newsletter → Targets

### Newsletter → X Thread
**Extract:** The argument structure — each section of the newsletter becomes a tweet.
**Adapt:** Compress each section to its core claim. Add transitions ("Here's why this matters:").
**Expected output:** 6–10 tweets.

### Newsletter → LinkedIn Post
**Extract:** The editorial angle — your take, not just the information.
**Adapt:** Open with your opinion first, support it with the newsletter's content.
**Expected output:** 600–1,500 chars.

### Newsletter → Instagram Carousel
**Extract:** Numbered frameworks, checklists, or how-to sequences.
**Adapt:** One step/item per slide. Visual hierarchy over text density.
**Expected output:** 5–8 slides.

### Newsletter → Reddit Post
**Extract:** Research findings, data points, contrarian takes.
**Adapt:** Same as blog → Reddit. Lead with value, strip brand, invite discussion.
**Expected output:** 300–600 words.

## Presentation → Targets

### Presentation → LinkedIn Carousel
**Extract:** The deck's slides directly — edit for social consumption.
**Adapt:** Remove company/product branding from main slides. Lead with the most striking insight. Each slide = self-contained.
**Expected output:** 8–15 slides.

### Presentation → X Thread
**Extract:** One key stat or claim per major section.
**Adapt:** Thread = abridged version of the talk. Final tweet = "Full deck in replies."
**Expected output:** 6–10 tweets.

### Presentation → YouTube Long-form
**Extract:** The full talk as a script.
**Adapt:** Record with visual aids showing the slides. Add B-roll or demos for sections that are "tell" without "show."
**Expected output:** 15–45 min video.

## Repurposing Sequence Strategy

For maximum output with minimum new content, use this order:

```
Long-form source
    ↓
Blog post (if not already written)
    ↓
LinkedIn post + X thread (same week)
    ↓
LinkedIn/Instagram carousel (following week)
    ↓
TikTok/YouTube Short (week 3)
    ↓
Community post/Reddit (ongoing as evergreen)
```

**Golden rule:** Change the format AND the angle. Don't just resize. Different platform = different reader need = different entry point into the same idea.

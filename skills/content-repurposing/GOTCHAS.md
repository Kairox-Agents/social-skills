# Gotchas — Content Repurposing

## 1. Watermark Cross-Contamination

**What happens:** Creator downloads their TikTok video and uploads it to Instagram Reels. The TikTok watermark is embedded. Instagram detects it and reduces distribution.

**The data:** Adam Mosseri (Head of Instagram) confirmed: Instagram won't recommend videos with TikTok or other platform watermarks to new audiences. TikTok does the same with Instagram Reels watermarks. (Source: Instagram official guidance, multiple Mosseri statements)

**How to avoid:** Always use the raw video file (exported from your editor) for each platform upload. Never download from one platform and upload to another. A 90-day cross-posting test by Socialync (Jan 2026) confirmed: zero penalties when using raw exports, but watermarked content consistently underperformed.

## 2. The "Same Caption Everywhere" Trap

**What happens:** Agent repurposes a LinkedIn post into 5 platforms by adjusting character count and hashtags but keeping essentially the same text. Each platform version reads like a diluted version of the original.

**Why it matters:** Each platform has different voice expectations, different algorithm signals, and different audience behavior. A LinkedIn post adapted for Reddit sounds promotional. A Reddit post adapted for Instagram sounds too casual. Text that works as a 2,000-char LinkedIn post becomes thin when squeezed into a 280-char tweet.

**How to avoid:** Extract the CORE INSIGHT from the source, then rebuild each platform version from scratch using that insight. The repurposing matrix exists for this reason — each source→target pair has different extraction and adaptation rules.

## 3. Forced Repurposing of Thin Content

**What happens:** Creator has a 500-word blog post and asks to repurpose it into 15 social posts across 5 platforms. The blog doesn't contain enough distinct ideas to support 15 quality posts, so the agent generates filler.

**Why it matters:** A source with 2 good ideas produces 2-3 good social posts, not 15. Padding to hit a number creates forgettable content that hurts the account's algorithmic standing.

**How to avoid:** Be honest about how many ideas the source contains. Count the distinct insights. Each social post needs at least one standalone insight. If the source has 3 insights, produce 3-5 posts, not 15.

## 4. Sequencing Mistakes

**What happens:** Creator publishes the detailed LinkedIn post first, then the X thread summarizing it. Followers who saw both feel like they're seeing the same thing twice.

**Why it matters:** Cross-platform audiences overlap. If 30% of your followers follow you on both X and LinkedIn, and you post the same idea on both platforms the same day, that 30% gets fatigued.

**The data:** Socialync's cross-posting analysis (Jan 2026) recommends: primary platform first, secondary 1-2 days later, tertiary 1 week later. Staggering prevents self-competition for your own audience's attention.

**How to avoid:** Use the cross-posting sequence guidelines. Space out by 1-2 days minimum between platforms for the same core idea. Post discovery formats first (Reels/TikTok), then conversation formats (X/Reddit), then authority formats (LinkedIn/YouTube).

## 5. Not Every Source Repurposes Well

**What happens:** Creator asks to repurpose a time-sensitive newsletter about a specific event. By the time it's adapted for 5 platforms and scheduled across 2 weeks, the event is old news.

**Why it matters:** Time-sensitive content has a window. News, event recaps, and trend commentary must be published quickly or not at all. Spreading them across a 2-week schedule defeats the purpose.

**How to avoid:** Flag time-sensitive content and prioritize speed over breadth. Publish to 2-3 platforms same day rather than 5 platforms over 2 weeks.

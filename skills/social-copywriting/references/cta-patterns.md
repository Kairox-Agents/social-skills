# CTA Patterns by Platform

> Every CTA should target a specific algorithm signal. Random "let me know what you think" is wasted real estate.

## Algorithm Signal → CTA Mapping

| Signal | Weight | Platforms Where It Matters Most |
|--------|--------|-------------------------------|
| Reply/Comment | Highest | X (27x like), LinkedIn (thread depth), Reddit (comment count) |
| Save/Bookmark | Very High | X (10x like), Instagram (key ranking signal), TikTok (save-to-like ratio) |
| Share/Repost | High | X (20x like), LinkedIn (extended reach), TikTok (share velocity) |
| Dwell Time | Medium-High | LinkedIn (scroll stop), Instagram (carousel swipes), YouTube (watch time) |
| Profile Click | Medium | LinkedIn (signals authority), Instagram (follow funnel) |

---

## X / Twitter CTAs

### Reply-Optimized (27x weight)
- "Which one do you disagree with? Reply below."
- "What would you add to this list?"
- "Drop your [thing] below. I'll reply to every one."
- "Agree or disagree? I want to hear it."
- "What's your version of this?"
- "Tell me I'm wrong."

### Bookmark-Optimized (10x weight)
- "Bookmark this for when you need it." *(use sparingly — overused)*
- "Save this thread. You'll need it."
- "Pin this for later."

### Repost-Optimized (20x weight)
- "Repost if this helped you."
- "Share this with someone who needs to hear it."
- *(Best CTA for reposts: make the content so good people repost without being asked)*

### Anti-CTAs for X
- ❌ "Like and retweet!" — feels desperate, algorithm doesn't reward it proportionally
- ❌ "Follow me for more" — weak signal, creates no engagement
- ❌ "Link in bio" — link suppression means fewer people see this post to begin with
- ❌ Generic "thoughts?" — too vague to prompt real replies

## LinkedIn CTAs

### Comment-Optimized (thread depth = key signal)
- "What's your experience with [topic]? Share below."
- "Agree or disagree? I'll engage with every comment."
- "Which of these resonates most with your [role]?"
- "What would you add? I'm genuinely curious."
- "Hot take or common sense? Tell me."
- "What's the [topic] lesson you learned the hard way?"

### Save-Optimized
- "Save this post → [menu icon] → Save"
- *(LinkedIn saves are underused; remind people the feature exists)*

### Profile Click-Optimized
- "I write about [topic] weekly. Hit Follow if that's your thing."
- *(Best done at END of high-value posts, not as the main CTA)*

### Anti-CTAs for LinkedIn
- ❌ "Like if you agree" — engagement bait, may trigger quality filter
- ❌ "Comment [emoji] for [thing]" — explicitly penalized by LinkedIn's dual encoder since 2024
- ❌ Engagement pods / reciprocal commenting — detectable, reduces reach
- ❌ "DM me for [freebie]" — moves engagement off-platform; better as a comment reply

---

## Reddit CTAs

### Comment-Optimized (only signal that matters)
- "Has anyone else run into this? What worked for you?"
- "Would love to hear how others handle [problem]."
- "Am I overthinking this, or is [thing] actually important?"
- "Curious how this compares to your experience."
- "What am I missing here?"

### Anti-CTAs for Reddit
- ❌ ANY self-promotional CTA — instant downvote magnet
- ❌ "Follow me" / "Subscribe" / "Check out my [thing]" — will get removed
- ❌ "Like this post" — Reddit doesn't work that way; sounds clueless
- ❌ Linking to your own content without genuine value-first framing

## Instagram CTAs

### Save-Optimized (strongest ranking signal)
- "Save this for your next [activity]."
- "📌 Save → share → use when you need it."
- "This is a save-worthy post. Come back to it."

### Comment-Optimized
- "Which slide was your favorite? Comment the number."
- "Tag someone who needs to see this."
- "What's your [topic] hot take? Drop it below."

### Share-Optimized
- "Send this to your [friend/coworker] who [situation]."
- "Share to your story if this hit home."

### Anti-CTAs for Instagram
- ❌ "Link in bio" as main CTA — low conversion, feels like an ad
- ❌ "Double tap if you agree" — outdated engagement bait
- ❌ Hashtag stuffing in the CTA line — distracts from the action

---

## TikTok CTAs

### Comment-Optimized (velocity matters in first hour)
- "Comment [word] if you want part 2."
- "Am I wrong? Tell me in the comments."
- "What would you do differently?"
- "Stitch this with your take."
- "Duet this if you disagree."

### Save-Optimized (save-to-like ratio = quality signal)
- "Save this — you'll forget otherwise."
- "Bookmark for when you actually need it."

### Share-Optimized
- "Send this to someone who [situation]."
- "Share this before it gets taken down." *(only if genuinely edgy content)*

### Anti-CTAs for TikTok
- ❌ "Like and follow" — feels inauthentic; doesn't match platform voice
- ❌ Long written CTAs — TikTok is audio/visual; CTA should be spoken naturally
- ❌ "Check the link in my bio" — TikTok suppresses content that drives off-platform

---

## YouTube CTAs

### Watch Time Optimized (strongest signal)
- "Stay until the end — the last one is the most important."
- "I saved the best for last."
- *(Pattern interrupt at 70% mark to retain viewers)*

### Comment-Optimized
- "Drop your [answer] in the comments. I read every one."
- "Which [option] would you pick? Comment A or B."
- "What should I cover next? Comment below."

### Subscribe-Optimized
- "If this helped, subscribe. I make one of these every [cadence]."
- *(Place after delivering value, never at the beginning)*

### Anti-CTAs for YouTube
- ❌ "Smash that like button" — overdone, creates viewer fatigue
- ❌ CTA before any value delivered — earns an instant click-away
- ❌ Multiple CTAs in the same breath — "like subscribe comment share" = noise

---

## Universal CTA Principles

1. **One CTA per post.** Multiple CTAs = no CTA. Pick the signal you want to amplify.
2. **Match the CTA to the content type.** Educational → save. Controversial → reply. Emotional → share.
3. **The best CTA is invisible.** Content so good people engage without being told.
4. **CTA placement matters.** On LinkedIn, CTA at the end. On TikTok, CTA spoken mid-video (people drop off at the end). On X, CTA in the last tweet of a thread.
5. **Rotate CTAs.** Using the same one every post trains your audience to ignore it.

# TikTok Viral Examples — Business/Founder Accounts
# Evidence quality: Public posts, engagement estimates from visible metrics (Jan-Mar 2026)
# Estimates marked with ~

## Example 1: POV + Screen Recording
**Script summary:** "POV: you finally look at your analytics and realize your most viral post took 5 minutes and your most 'polished' post flopped. Camera shows creator going from confused face → checking phone → zooming in on analytics screen → throwing hands up → cutting to 'what I learned' talking head."
**Caption:** "The algorithm doesn't care about your effort 💀 #contentcreator #socialmedia #marketing"
**Format:** Selfie cam + screen recording, 47 seconds
**Engagement:** ~180K views, ~12K likes, ~890 comments, ~2,400 saves, ~1,100 shares
**Date:** Feb 2026
**Why it worked:**
- Relatable frustration = instant identification
- Visual proof (actual analytics on screen) = authenticity
- Short enough to loop (47s) — loop rate boosts algorithmic score
- Save-worthy because the lesson is reusable
- "💀" emoji signals self-deprecating humor = right tone for TikTok

## Example 2: Talking Head + Text Overlay Listicle
**Script summary:** "Three things I wish someone told me before I started my business: One — your first 100 customers are worth more than your next 10,000. Two — the unsexy stuff IS the strategy — invoicing, follow-ups, SOPs. Three — you'll feel behind every single day. That's not a bug, it's the game."
**Caption:** "Year 3 hits different 🫠 #entrepreneur #startup #businesstips"
**Format:** Direct-to-camera, text overlays for each point, 52 seconds
**Engagement:** ~320K views, ~28K likes, ~1,600 comments, ~5,200 saves, ~2,100 shares
**Date:** Jan 2026
**Why it worked:**
- "Three things I wish someone told me" = proven hook framework
- Each point is counterintuitive enough to stop and think
- "Year 3 hits different" = specificity (not generic advice from day 1)
- High save-to-like ratio (5.2K/28K = 18%) signals utility content
- Comments full of "number 2 🙏" = people tagging their own experience

## Example 3: Green Screen + Hot Take
**Script summary:** Creator uses green screen showing a LinkedIn post that says "hustle culture is dead." Creator reacts: "Nah. Hustle culture isn't dead. Performative hustle culture is dead. There's a massive difference. The people who are actually winning right now? They're working hard, they're just not posting about it. The flex shifted from 'look how hard I work' to 'look how smart I work.' But make no mistake — they're still grinding."
**Caption:** "The quiet grinders are eating 🤫 #hustleculture #entrepreneurship #business"
**Format:** Green screen reaction, 58 seconds
**Engagement:** ~450K views, ~35K likes, ~2,800 comments, ~3,900 saves, ~2,600 shares
**Date:** Mar 2026
**Why it worked:**
- Green screen + reaction to another platform's content = TikTok-native format
- Nuanced take (not just "agree" or "disagree" — reframes the debate)
- Hook in first 3 seconds ("Nah.") immediately creates tension
- Comment section became a debate (algorithmic gold — comments drive reach)
- Share-worthy because people want to say "THIS" to their friends

## Example 4: Storytime + "Wait for It"
**Script summary:** "Story time. A client DM'd me and said 'I need you to make me go viral.' I said 'what's your product?' They said 'we sell B2B accounting software.' I said 'okay, who's your customer?' They said 'CFOs at mid-market companies.' I said 'and what's the most interesting thing about your product?' [long pause] 'It does accounting.' [beat] So here's what we actually did... [cuts to results: 2M organic views in 60 days for an accounting software company]"
**Caption:** "B2B can go viral. You just need to stop being boring about it. #b2bmarketing #viral #contentmarketing"
**Format:** Talking head with jump cuts, 73 seconds
**Engagement:** ~280K views, ~19K likes, ~1,100 comments, ~4,800 saves, ~1,500 shares
**Date:** Feb 2026
**Why it worked:**
- Storytelling hook with escalating humor
- Subverts expectations ("accounting software" → "2M views")
- "Wait for it" tension keeps watch time high (73s full watch = strong signal)
- Save rate very high (4.8K saves) — people bookmarking the "boring product → viral" playbook
- Duet/stitch bait — other creators reacted with their own B2B stories

## Example 5: Quick Tip + Social Proof
**Script summary:** "One thing that 10x'd my LinkedIn growth: I stopped writing posts. I started writing replies. For 30 days I replied to 50 posts a day from big accounts in my niche. Result? My follower count went from 2K to 11K. My DMs went from empty to 5-10 inbound leads per week. The algorithm rewards engagement. So I became the most engaged person in my niche."
**Caption:** "This cost $0 and took 30 min/day 👀 #linkedin #socialmediatips #growthhack"
**Format:** Direct-to-camera, fast cuts, 41 seconds
**Engagement:** ~520K views, ~42K likes, ~3,200 comments, ~8,100 saves, ~3,500 shares
**Date:** Mar 2026
**Why it worked:**
- Specific, actionable, and free — the trifecta for saves
- Social proof (2K → 11K, real timeline, real metric)
- Cross-platform advice (TikTok audience learning about LinkedIn) = novelty
- 41 seconds = high loop potential
- Save-to-like ratio (8.1K/42K = 19%) = exceptional utility signal
- Comment section full of "does this actually work?" = engagement loop

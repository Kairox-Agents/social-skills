# AI-Tell Checklist

> Patterns that make content read as AI-generated. Use this as a QA gate before publishing.

## Universal AI Tells (All Platforms)

### Vocabulary Red Flags
These words/phrases are statistically overrepresented in AI output. Their presence doesn't guarantee AI origin, but stacking 3+ in one post is a strong signal.

| AI Tell | Why It's a Tell | Human Alternative |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| "In today's digital landscape" | No human starts a social post like this | Cut entirely — start with the point |
| "Leverage" (as verb) | Overused in AI business writing | "Use", "try", "apply" |
| "Delve into" | AI favorite; humans rarely say this | "Look at", "dig into", "explore" |
| "Game-changer" | Generic hype word | Be specific: "This cut our churn by 30%" |
| "Unlock [potential/growth]" | AI motivational-speak | "Get", "reach", "build" |
| "Navigate the complexities" | Nobody talks like this | "Figure out", "deal with" |
| "It's worth noting that" | Filler phrase AI uses for transitions | Cut it — just state the thing |
| "In conclusion" | Essay structure in a social post | Just end. The last line IS the conclusion. |
| "Firstly... Secondly... Lastly" | Over-structured enumeration | "First... Also... And:" |
| "Robust" | AI-corporate favorite | "Strong", "solid", "reliable" |
| "Seamless/seamlessly" | Almost never used in casual speech | "Smooth", "easy", "without friction" |
| "Groundbreaking" | Hyperbolic, unearned | Describe what makes it different specifically |
| "Embark on [a journey]" | AI narrative cliché | "Start", "begin", "try" |
| "Foster [collaboration/growth]" | Corporate AI-speak | "Build", "encourage", "create" |
| "Harness [the power of]" | Overused AI intensifier | "Use" |
| "Comprehensive" | AI padding word | Cut it — just describe what it covers |
| "Moreover" / "Furthermore" | Essay connectives, not social voice | Start a new sentence or use "also" |
| "At the end of the day" | Cliché AI uses to wrap up | Just make the point directly |
| "Let's dive in" | AI thread/article opener | Cut or replace with a specific hook |

### Structural AI Tells

| Pattern | Why It's a Tell | Fix |
|---------|----------------|-----|
| Every sentence a different paragraph | AI over-formats for "readability" | Group related ideas; vary paragraph length |
| Emoji at the start of every bullet | 🚀 📊 💡 🔥 pattern = AI formatting | Use emoji sparingly and inconsistently |
| Perfect parallel structure in lists | AI makes every item grammatically identical | Vary list item structure naturally |
| "Hook + 5 numbered points + CTA" every time | Formulaic structure repetition | Vary formats: some posts are 2 sentences, some are stories |
| Overly balanced "on one hand / on the other" | AI hedges by default | Take a position. Be opinionated. |
| Starting 3+ sentences with "This" | AI's favorite sentence starter | Vary: "The result?", "What happened:", "So..." |
| Lists of exactly 5 or 10 items | AI defaults to round numbers | 7, 11, 3 — odd/specific numbers feel more human |

### Tone AI Tells

| Pattern | Why It's a Tell | Fix |
|---------|----------------|-----|
| Relentlessly positive | AI avoids negativity by default | Include honest frustrations, mixed feelings |
| No personal details | AI can't invent specific memories | Add a real detail: time, place, person, feeling |
| Generic examples | "For example, a company might..." | "When we did this at [company], [specific result]" |
| Perfect grammar throughout | Humans make deliberate stylistic choices | Fragments. Starting with "And." Casual punctuation. |
| No contractions | "It is important" vs "it's important" | Use contractions — they're how people actually write |
| Excessive hedging | "It may be beneficial to consider..." | "Do this." or "This works because..." |
| Thanking the reader | "Thank you for reading!" | Skip — your content should earn attention, not beg for it |

## Platform-Specific AI Tells

### X / Twitter
- **AI tell:** Thread where every tweet starts with a number + emoji (1/ 🔑, 2/ 💡, 3/ 🚀)
  - **Fix:** Number without emoji, or skip numbers entirely
- **AI tell:** Thread ending with "If you found this valuable, retweet the first tweet"
  - **Fix:** End with a genuine question or provocative closing thought
- **AI tell:** Overly polished single tweets — no abbreviations, perfect punctuation
  - **Fix:** Write like you text. "ngl this changed how I think about pricing"
- **AI tell:** Using "🧵" emoji to signal thread
  - **Fix:** If the content is good, people will read it. Don't label it.

### LinkedIn
- **AI tell:** Opening with "I'm thrilled to announce..." or "Excited to share..."
  - **Fix:** Open with the WHAT, not your emotion about it
- **AI tell:** Every paragraph is exactly 1-2 sentences with line breaks
  - **Fix:** Vary rhythm — some single lines, some 3-4 sentence paragraphs
- **AI tell:** Ending with "Agree? ♻️ Repost to share with your network"
  - **Fix:** Ask a genuine, specific question
- **AI tell:** Using 10+ hashtags
  - **Fix:** 2-3 relevant hashtags maximum, placed at the end
- **AI tell:** "What are your thoughts?" as CTA
  - **Fix:** Ask something specific: "What's the hardest part of [topic] for you?"

### Reddit
- **AI tell:** Starting with "Hey [subreddit], I wanted to share..."
  - **Fix:** Jump straight to the content
- **AI tell:** Bullet-point heavy, overly organized
  - **Fix:** Write conversationally, like you're explaining to a friend
- **AI tell:** No typos, no informal language, no slang
  - **Fix:** Match the subreddit's communication style
- **AI tell:** Self-promotional framing disguised as value
  - **Fix:** Give value with zero expectation. If it's good, people will check your profile.

### Instagram
- **AI tell:** Caption reads like a blog post paragraph
  - **Fix:** Short punchy lines. Line breaks. Conversational.
- **AI tell:** 30 hashtags at the end
  - **Fix:** 5-10 relevant ones. Mix popular + niche.
- **AI tell:** Carousel slides with identical formatting and word count
  - **Fix:** Vary slide density. Some slides = 3 words. Some = 20.

### TikTok
- **AI tell:** Script reads like it was written, not spoken
  - **Fix:** Record yourself saying it naturally, then transcribe THAT
- **AI tell:** Perfect sentence structure in captions
  - **Fix:** Lowercase, casual punctuation, platform slang ("ngl", "fr", "iykyk")
- **AI tell:** Overly structured "intro → 3 points → conclusion"
  - **Fix:** Start in the middle. End abruptly. TikTok rewards energy, not structure.

### YouTube
- **AI tell:** Title is generic ("5 Tips for Better Marketing")
  - **Fix:** Specific + curiosity: "The marketing strategy that took us from $0 to $50K/mo"
- **AI tell:** Description is a wall of keywords
  - **Fix:** Write 2-3 sentences about the video, then timestamps
- **AI tell:** Thumbnail text is the title repeated
  - **Fix:** Thumbnail = emotional hook (face + short text). Title = specific promise.

## The 3-Read Test

Before posting, read your content three times:
1. **First read:** Does this sound like something I'd actually say out loud?
2. **Second read:** Could this have been written by anyone, or does it have MY fingerprint?
3. **Third read:** If I saw this from someone else, would I engage or scroll past?

If any answer is no, rewrite.

## Quick Self-Check (Yes = Probably AI)
- [ ] Contains 3+ words from the vocabulary red flags list
- [ ] Every paragraph is the same length
- [ ] No personal details, anecdotes, or specific examples
- [ ] Perfectly balanced and hedged — never takes a strong position
- [ ] Uses emoji as bullet points (🔑 💡 🚀 📊 pattern)
- [ ] CTA is generic ("thoughts?" / "agree?")
- [ ] Could apply to any industry — no niche specificity
- [ ] Zero humor, sarcasm, or personality
- [ ] Reads like a LinkedIn article summary, not a social post

**Score:** 0–2 checked = human-passing. 3–5 = edit needed. 6+ = rewrite from scratch.

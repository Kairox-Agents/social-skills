# LinkedIn Algorithm Deep Dive (March 2026)

## High-confidence signals

- Dwell-time and meaningful comment quality are strong ranking inputs.
- Early engagement quality (not just count) affects secondary distribution.
- Profile-topic relevance matters: content is routed to likely-interested audiences.
- Native formats (text + document/carousel + native video) generally outperform external-link-first posts.

## Medium-confidence claims

- Save behavior is a high-value quality indicator for educational posts.
- Document/carousel format continues to perform strongly for B2B education.
- Generic AI-sounding content appears to underperform due to quality filtering + audience fatigue.

## Practical optimization rules

1. First 2-3 lines must force "see more" click.
2. Use one clear thesis + one concrete lesson.
3. Write for comments with substance, not vanity reactions.
4. Use examples and numbers.
5. Keep external links to comments or as secondary action.

## Anti-patterns

- Corporate press-release voice
- Engagement bait ("comment YES")
- Buzzword stacking
- Topic mismatch with profile authority
